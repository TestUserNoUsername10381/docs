## Is Forge unbreakable?

No obfuscator is unbreakable. Forge is designed to make casual decompilation, signature matching, and pattern-based deobfuscation impractical — not impossible. With enough time and skill any virtualization can be reversed; Forge raises the cost dramatically.

## What languages does Forge support?

Lua and Luau only. Lua 5.1–5.4, LuaJIT, Luau (Roblox).

## Will my script still run normally?

Yes. Forge preserves runtime semantics. Expect a runtime overhead proportional to the protection strength you choose.

## How much overhead does the VM add?

Roughly 15–100% slower depending on preset (Balanced → VM Extreme). Tight inner loops are most affected.

## Can I deobfuscate Forge output?

Forge does not provide deobfuscation tooling for any obfuscator, including itself. Forge is a one-way protection tool.

## Does Forge collect or transmit user data from my script?

No. Forge does not inject telemetry, trackers, or analytics into protected scripts. Your script's behavior is preserved exactly.

## Can I use Forge in CI?

Yes — use the [CLI](/reference/cli) or the [HTTP API](/reference/api) with an API key.

## What's the difference between presets and Output Length?

- **Presets** control which protection modules run.
- **Output Length** controls how much padding, junk, and structural complexity is added.

They are independent.

## How do tickets work?

Each build consumes tickets based on protection strength. See [Tickets](/account/tickets).

## What happens if my build runs out of tickets mid-month?

New builds are blocked until your next reset. Existing protected scripts continue to work normally.
